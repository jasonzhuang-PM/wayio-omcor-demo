The Problem

Outdoors has GPS. Indoors is a blind spot.

Until now, indoor positioning has been too expensive, too slow, or too fragile, which kept the market from ever scaling.

💰

High hardware cost

UWB, Bluetooth beacons, cameras and the like often mean a million-dollar hardware build for a single venue.

🐌

Long deployment

Months of installation, calibration and tuning, often requiring downtime to coordinate.

🧱

Fragile in the field

Metal, crowds, walls and lighting easily throw signals off in factories and hospitals.

🔧

Heavy maintenance

Every renovation means a rebuild, and batteries and devices need constant inspection.

How it works

One AI core, built on the signals you already have

omcor uses AI models to fuse the signals already present in your venue and compute position and floor directly, so there's no separate hardware to roll out just for positioning. Most venues already have coverage; you only need to reinforce the existing network in the few corners where reception is weak.

People spend roughly 80% of their time indoors, yet indoor positioning has long been held back by hardware cost and installation barriers. FAQ

FAQ

The questions we hear most about omcor indoor positioning.

Do we need to install dedicated positioning hardware?

No. omcor reuses your venue's existing Wi-Fi/5G/Bluetooth/UWB signals. Most venues already have coverage; you only reinforce the existing network in the few sparse spots.

What's the positioning accuracy?

Typically 2–3 meters, with floor detection, outputting latitude, longitude and floor information.

How long until it goes live?

Deployment runs about 3,300 m²/day, and most venues produce usable position output within days, or even the same day.

Which venues is it suited for?

It already runs in international airports, large hospitals, electronics manufacturing campuses and university/corporate campuses, with one engine replicating quickly across venues.
